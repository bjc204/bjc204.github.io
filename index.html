Hello! I am Brendan Clark, a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Cornell University studying impacts to crops and natural vegetation from climate change and stratospheric aerosol climate intervention using global numerical models.

Climate intervention would be the large scale manipulation of the Earth system to counteract climate change impacts by reflecting a small portion of incoming sunlight. Placing reflective sulfate aerosols in the stratosphere would be an attempt to mimic large volcanic eruptions and temporarily cool Earth's surface.

However, the impacts of doing such a large scale climate intervention are still relativley unknown. What would happen to agriculture, human health, biodiversity, wildfires, water quality, or fisheries? Scientists can try to answer these questions using sophisticated Earth system models.

I believe that answering these questions can help inform future policy decisions about climate intervention, and could introduce risks of climate intervention that were not previously considered.

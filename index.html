Hello! I am Brendan Clark, a recent PhD graduate at Rutgers University working with Alan Robock's group studying impacts to crops from climate change and stratospheric aerosol climate intervention technologies using global climate and crop models.

Climate intervention technologies are the large scale manipulation of the Earth system to counteract climate change impacts. Placing reflective sulfate aerosols in the stratosphere would be an attempt to mimic large volcanic eruptions and temporarily cool Earth's surface

However, the impacts of doing such a large scale climate intervention are still relativley unknown. What would happen to agriculture, human health, biodiversity, wildfires, water quality, or fisheries? Scientists can try to answer these questions using sophisticated Earth system models.

I believe that answering these questions can help inform future policy decisions about climate intervention, and could introduce risks of climate intervention that were not previously considered.
